What to Include in a debt collection Letter?

A debt collection letter is a formal document that is sent to a debtor who has not made a payment on their debt. The letter should include the following information:

  • The amount of money that the debtor owes.
  • The original due date for the payment.
  • A new due date for the payment, which should be as soon as possible.
  • Instructions on how to pay the debt.
  • A statement that the debtor has the right to dispute the debt if they believe that it is not valid.
  • A friendly but firm reminder that payment is due.
  • A note that the creditor would like to continue doing business with the debtor, but that payment is required in order to do so.
  • A warning that legal action may be taken if the debt is not paid.

The language used in a debt collection letter should be clear and concise. It should be polite, but firm. The letter should not make threats or use abusive language.
